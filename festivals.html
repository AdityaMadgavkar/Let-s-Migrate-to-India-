Festivals in India


1. National Festivals                                   
  • India celebrates 3 national festivals, namely Independence Day (15 August), Republic Day (26 January), and Gandhi Jayanti (2 October)
  • National holidays play important role in public life of any nation. This is a common cause for all Indian nationals to celebrate the day so is the basis for national integration.


2. Hindu Festivals                                       
    1. Ganesh Chaturthi
    2. Dussehra
    3. Diwali


3. Muslim Festivals                                     
    1. Ramzan
    2. Id-Ul-Fitr
    3. Moharam


4. Christian Festivals                                  
    1. Christmas
    2. Good Friday


5. Other Festivals                                        
    1. Guru Nanak Jayanti (Sikh)
    2. Paryushan (Jain)
    3. Buddha Jayanti (Buddhist)
    4. Nowruz (Parsi)
